Jeep

UX HMI Lead
Leading UX Design for Iconic Off-Road Digital Instrumentation

Case Study

Overview

As the UX HMI Lead assigned to the Jeep program within Stellantis, the focus was to design, refine, and implement Human-Machine Interface (HMI) elements specifically for the cluster displays of the iconic Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator models. This role involved collaborating with various teams, including Vehicle Feature Owners, Software Factory, UX Design (UXD), and others, to create a seamless, user-centric experience that aligns with Jeep's brand values of ruggedness, reliability, and intuitive functionality.

Role and Responsibilities

- Primary Scope: Defining interactions, indications, and functionality within the digital instrument clusters for both the Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator models. This included the development of visual layouts, information prioritization, and interactive components within the cluster.
- Cross-functional Collaboration: Worked alongside Vehicle Feature Owners to ensure that the HMI elements align with specific features such as ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems), off-road capabilities, and safety protocols. Collaborated with the Software Factory to ensure seamless integration of design with software functionality, ensuring that user feedback and behavioral data were considered in iterative updates.
- Tool Utilization: Leveraged HMI documentation, ADAS protocols, and other UX research methodologies to define feature priorities and test usability. Used design tools for rapid prototyping and feedback loops to validate concepts before implementation.

Challenges and Solutions

1. Challenge: Maintaining Jeep's brand identity of rugged and straightforward functionality while incorporating advanced digital interactions.
- Solution: Emphasized a minimalistic design approach that aligns with Jeep's adventurous and utilitarian brand ethos. Prioritized essential driving information (e.g., speed, fuel level, ADAS indicators) in accessible locations while ensuring the visibility of off-road metrics, like incline and pitch, that Jeep owners value.
2. Challenge: Balancing innovation with usability, especially given the off-road usage profile of the Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator.
- Solution: Conducted user testing in simulated off-road environments to validate the cluster's readability and functionality under various driving conditions. Adjusted font sizes, icon placements, and contrast levels to ensure legibility even in direct sunlight or bumpy conditions.
3. Challenge: Coordinating with multiple teams while keeping timelines on track.
- Solution: Created a structured communication protocol and established regular check-ins with stakeholders from the Vehicle Feature team, Software Factory, and UXD. Used agile methodologies to keep iterations quick and focused, aligning each team's input with project milestones.

Outcome and Impact

- Enhanced User Experience: The final HMI design delivered a seamless, brand-aligned experience for Jeep users, enhancing their interaction with essential vehicle features without overwhelming them.
- Improved Readability and Accessibility: The clusters provided intuitive feedback for ADAS and off-road functions, ensuring that drivers could easily access information, whether on city roads or rugged trails.
- Cross-functional Success: The project fostered a robust, collaborative environment between teams within Stellantis, demonstrating the effectiveness of integrated UX HMI practices across disciplines.

Lessons Learned

This project underscored the importance of aligning design with brand identity, especially for a vehicle brand like Jeep, which carries strong user expectations. Additionally, the need for constant communication and collaboration across different departments proved essential in balancing technical feasibility with user experience goals. This case study demonstrates how an effective UX HMI Lead can bring together different functions within an organization to deliver a product that resonates with users and meets both brand and functionality requirements.
