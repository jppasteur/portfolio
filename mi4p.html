MI4P

UXUI Designer:
Creating a Design system for an e-commerce in the financial industry.

My Role:

At MI4P, a Management Information System in the North American sector, I contributed in three core aspects: crafting a comprehensive User Interface (UI) kit to strengthen the digital brand, developing an integrated design system by merging code components, and serving as a UI designer. These efforts ensured efficient development processes, higher scalability rates, and a cohesive, user-centric interface.

Tools:

To address the challenges caused by rapid growth and inconsistent identity documents, we needed to establish a unified design system. We held a meeting with the Design System Principal at Oracle to gain insights on tackling this challenge. Based on the meeting, we outlined a roadmap for creating our own design system. The tools and processes we employed included cataloging components, documenting rules and guidelines, linking the UI Kit in Figma, and organizing Scss class components.

Design:

Establishing a design system was crucial to addressing inconsistency issues, accelerating scalability rates, and building a solid foundation for component development. Alongside two front-end developers and two UX designers, I led the initiative to create this system. We meticulously documented every component used in the application, ensuring comprehensive guidelines and easy access to documentation.

Key Takeaway:

The implementation of the design system had remarkable results. We reduced the scalability time from a two-and-a-half-month learning curve to just three weeks, allowing new developers and designers to become fully operational in a third of the time. This experience highlighted the importance of a well-structured design system in achieving operational efficiency and high user satisfaction.
